Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 13, 2026

Hide the Word is a Bible memorization app built by Griffin Barnard. Your privacy matters, and this policy is written in plain language so you know exactly what happens with your data.

What stays on your device

All of your memorization progress, review history, preferences, and draft sessions are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. This data is never sent to any server controlled by us.

iCloud and shared plans

If you use the shared plans feature, the following data is synced through Apple's CloudKit service:

  • Your display name, bio, favorite verse, and avatar seed (your public profile)
  • Your plan progress (current day, completed days, streak)

This data is stored in Apple's iCloud infrastructure under your Apple ID. We do not operate any servers and do not have access to your iCloud data. Apple's privacy practices apply: apple.com/privacy

ESV Bible text

When you select the ESV translation, the app sends verse references (e.g., "John 3:16") to the ESV API (api.esv.org) operated by Crossway to retrieve passage text. No personal information is included in these requests.

What we don't do

  • We do not collect analytics or usage data
  • We do not show advertisements
  • We do not use third-party tracking SDKs
  • We do not require account creation
  • We do not sell, share, or transfer your data to anyone

Notifications

If you enable daily reminders, notification scheduling is handled entirely on-device. No push notification servers are involved.

Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The shared plans feature uses iCloud, which is governed by the Apple ID holder's settings.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.
